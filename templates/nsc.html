History

The Sinclair formula has been the stardard for comparing lifters in different weight classes since 1980. Roy Sinclair, a Canadian mathematician who loved and dedicated a big part of his life to weightlifting, came up with the formula. His formula allows for the comparison of two lifters by projecting how much each lifter would be able to if he were to move up to the heaviest weight class.

Though a magnificent method, the Sinclair formula only takes into consideration the body weight of the world record holder for the heaviest of all weight classes. It made sense at the time, since back then we didn't have the computational power we now have.

Reinvention

The Nel-Sinclair Coefficient is more of a revised version of the Sinclair formula. By approximating the best logarithmic curve that best fits all eight world records, we can now compare different lifters based on how far off they are from the curve. The NSC then, is more or less, a measure of how far off one is from being the best in the world.
