Can Computers predict where you'll be next year, as a lifter?

Progress is difficult to predict, because for example a lifter we'll probably get injured at least twice a year and this will result in a setback from his training. The idea is that, such conditions apply to all lifters and when we develop a model with all the data, things like injuries show up implicitly in the form of a decline in performance or the amount of times the person competed that year.

How it works:

Normalyzing the data by the NSC, allows for a better comparison of lifters by taking the body weight out of the equation. After testing a few models, 'KnearestKeighbors' was chosen based on its more accurate precision. Linear models fail to give reasonable predictions and it makes sense, since as we know, progress in weightlifting is not at all linear.

The model seems to work best for lifters with NSC coefficient in the range of -250 to -150. It works best for lifters hose data from previous competitions is already on the data base.
