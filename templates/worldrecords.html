Will the 56kg total record will ever be broken again?

Halil Mutlu (Bulgary) established the current record of 305kg in 1998. The closest anyone (other than Mutlu himself) has ever gotten to it was 302kg.

By Using the unsupervised Machine Learning method 'Naive Bayes', It is highly improbable (less than 1%) for the 56kg total record to be broken this year.

One data point. That's all.

The 85kg weight class was inagurated in 1998, in april of that same year, Zhang Yong (China) established the first and only so far, 'Clean and Jerk' world record with 218kg.

Similarly, in 1999, Akakios Kakiasvilis (Greece), established the 'Snatch' world record with 210kg at a body weight of 94kg.

Still not enough data

Another world record that has been standing for a while now is the 210 'Clean and Jerk' at the 77kg weight class, established by Oleg Perepetchenov (Russia), in 2001.

An Ever Evolving Sport

In 1973, the sport of Olympic Weightlifting changed its format, from three competition lifts to two. Getting rid of the 'Press' and only leaving the 'Snatch' and the 'Clean and Jerk'.

Weight classes have also change a lot through time. The 56kg weight class has been one of the most consistent ones.
